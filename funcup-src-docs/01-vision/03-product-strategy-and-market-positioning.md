# ├ 03 Product Strategy & Market Positioning   
**Version:** Draft 1   
**Purpose:** Strategic positioning of funcup for founders, investors and early partners.   
 --- 
# 1. The Core Problem   
Specialty coffee has experienced global growth over the last decade, but the **consumer data layer of the industry is almost nonexistent**.   
Today:   
- Consumers often **do not know what coffee they are drinking** beyond the label.   
- Roasters have **almost no structured feedback from consumers**.   
- Coffee discovery happens across fragmented channels: Instagram, blogs, cafés, packaging.   
   
Unlike other taste-driven industries, coffee lacks a **global digital interface between product and consumer experience**.   
Examples from other industries:   
| Industry | Platform |
|:---------|:---------|
|     Beer |  Untappd |
|     Wine |   Vivino |
|    Music |   Shazam |
|  Fitness |   Strava |

Coffee does not yet have an equivalent system that connects:   
```
product identity
consumer experience
community data
```
This is the gap funcup addresses.   
 --- 
# 2. The Core Insight   
Every bag of specialty coffee already contains a **physical identifier**: the product label.   
But there is no **universal digital identity for coffee products**.   
funcup introduces:   
```
QR → Coffee Identity → Consumer Experience
```
This simple mechanism enables a powerful system:   
- every coffee can be identified   
- every consumer experience can be recorded   
- every roaster can receive structured feedback.   
 --- 
   
# 3. Product Category   
funcup is best described as a **Coffee Discovery Platform**.   
More specifically:   
```
Product identity layer for coffee
+ consumer tasting network
+ discovery platform
```
In functional terms the product sits at the intersection of three categories.   
|                Category |                         Role |
|:------------------------|:-----------------------------|
|      Discovery platform | explore coffees and roasters |
|      Experience tracker |          log coffee tastings |
| Product identity system |            QR identification |

The closest analog is **Shazam for coffee**.   
 --- 
# 4. Why This Product Can Win   
There are three structural reasons why funcup has strong potential.   
 --- 
## 4.1 Coffee is becoming a product of discovery   
Specialty coffee consumers increasingly care about:   
- origin   
- processing   
- variety   
- roasting style   
- brewing methods   
   
This shift is supported by industry research. For example, the **Specialty Coffee Association (SCA) and Square’s “Future of Coffee Report”** highlights that modern coffee consumers increasingly value transparency about origin, processing methods, and roasting profiles (see: [https://squareup.com/us/en/the-bottom-line/reports/future-of-coffee](https://squareup.com/us/en/the-bottom-line/reports/future-of-coffee)). Similarly, the **National Coffee Association (NCA) National Coffee Data Trends report** shows growing consumer interest in specialty coffee attributes such as single-origin sourcing, brewing methods, and quality differentiation (see: [https://www.ncausa.org/Industry-Resources/Market-Research/NCDT](https://www.ncausa.org/Industry-Resources/Market-Research/NCDT)). Studies by **Allegra World Coffee Portal** also indicate that specialty coffee drinkers are more engaged with product information and are actively seeking knowledge about how coffee is produced and prepared (see: [https://www.worldcoffeeportal.com/Latest/InsightAnalysis/2023/September/Specialty-coffee-consumers-seek-transparency-and](https://www.worldcoffeeportal.com/Latest/InsightAnalysis/2023/September/Specialty-coffee-consumers-seek-transparency-and)).   
However, most of this information is currently **locked inside packaging or niche knowledge**.   
funcup makes this knowledge accessible in seconds.   
```
scan → understand → experience
```
 --- 
## 4.2 Roasters need consumer insight   
Roasters today rely mainly on:   
- café feedback   
- wholesale partners   
- online reviews.   
   
They rarely know:   
- how their coffee is brewed at home   
- how consumers perceive taste   
- which coffees become favorites.   
   
funcup creates the first **structured feedback loop between roasters and drinkers**.   
 --- 
## 4.3 Coffee discovery is fragmented   
Consumers currently discover coffee through:   
- cafés   
- social media   
- friends   
- packaging.   
   
But there is no **central place where coffee discovery accumulates over time**.   
funcup can become that place.   
 --- 
# 5. The Core Product Loop   
The entire product is designed around one loop:   
```
Roaster prints QR
↓
Consumer scans coffee
↓
Consumer logs tasting
↓
Community data grows
↓
Discovery improves
↓
More consumers join
↓
More roasters adopt QR
```
This loop creates a **two-sided network effect**.   
 --- 
# 6. Target Users   
funcup focuses on three early user groups.   
 --- 
## 6.1 Specialty Coffee Enthusiasts   
These users already:   
- buy specialty beans   
- experiment with brewing   
- discuss coffee online.   
   
For them funcup becomes a **coffee journal + discovery tool**.   
 --- 
## 6.2 Curious Coffee Drinkers   
Consumers who enjoy good coffee but lack deep knowledge.   
funcup helps them:   
- understand coffee   
- learn brewing   
- explore new roasters.   
 --- 
   
## 6.3 Roasters   
Roasters gain:   
- a digital product page   
- consumer analytics   
- a storytelling platform.   
 --- 
   
# 7. Competitive Landscape   
No existing platform combines **product identification + tasting data + discovery**.   
However, several adjacent products exist.   
|       Product |       Category |              Limitation |
|:--------------|:---------------|:------------------------|
|       Untappd | Beer discovery |    not product identity |
|        Vivino |  Wine scanning | focused on retail wines |
| BeanConqueror | Coffee logging |    niche and non-social |
|     Instagram |      Discovery |       unstructured data |

funcup differs because the **QR code creates a universal product identity**.   
 --- 
# 8. Why This Has Not Been Solved Yet   
Three reasons explain why the problem still exists.   
 --- 
## 8.1 Coffee products change frequently   
Coffee is not a static product.   
Variables include:   
- harvest   
- processing   
- roasting batch.   
   
Traditional product databases struggle with this variability.   
funcup solves this using **batch-based QR identity**.   
 --- 
## 8.2 Sensory language is complex   
Flavor descriptions like:   
- bergamot   
- stone fruit   
- jasmine   
   
can intimidate new users.   
funcup introduces **progressive sensory levels**.   
 --- 
## 8.3 The industry lacked a simple interface   
Previous attempts focused on:   
- databases   
- expert cupping tools.   
   
funcup focuses on **the simplest interaction possible**:   
```
scan coffee
```
 --- 
# 9. Strategic Positioning   
funcup should position itself as:   
```
The discovery layer of specialty coffee.
```
Or more simply:   
```
Discover the coffee you drink.
```
This positioning emphasizes:   
- curiosity   
- experience   
- discovery.   
 --- 
   
# 10. Long-Term Vision   
If successful, funcup can become the **global consumer data layer for coffee**.   
Future possibilities include:   
- global coffee discovery map   
- personalized coffee recommendations   
- flavor preference models   
- direct consumer insights for roasters.   
   
Ultimately, funcup could become:   
```
the reference system for how coffee is experienced worldwide.
```
 --- 
# 11. North Star Metric   
The most important metric for the product is:   
```
coffees logged per user
```
Why?   
Because it reflects:   
- engagement   
- discovery   
- product value.   
 --- 
   
# 12. Summary   
funcup addresses a fundamental gap in the specialty coffee ecosystem.   
The platform introduces:   
- digital product identity for coffee   
- structured consumer tasting data   
- a global discovery platform.   
   
By connecting **roasters, products and consumer experiences**, funcup has the potential to become the central interface of specialty coffee discovery.
